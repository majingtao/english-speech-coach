# Cambridge Flyers A2 Speaking — Official Question Bank

Source: *Go Flyers Teacher's Notes* (H.Q. Mitchell & Marileni Malkogianni, MM Publications, 2018 YLE Revised Edition)

---

## HOW TO USE

At the start of every session, run **Part 0** (warm-up + test selection), then proceed through **Parts 1–4** of the chosen test using the **exact examiner scripts** below.

**Examiner rules:**
- Use ONLY the scripted words. Do not paraphrase.
- Wait for the student's response before continuing.
- If silent, use only the listed prompt questions.
- Do NOT correct during the test. Give feedback after Part 4 ends.

---

## PART 0 — Warm-Up (Same for All Tests)

**Examiner says:**
> *"Hello. My name's [examiner's name]."*

> *"What's your name?"*

> *"What's your surname?"*

> *"How old are you?"*

> *"Which test would you like to do today — Test 1, Test 2, Test 3, Test 4, or Test 5?"*

**Minimum response expected:** Hello. / (first name) / (surname) / (age) / Test (number)

**Alternative prompts if no response:**
> *"What's your family name?"* / *"Are you twelve years old?"*

## TEST 1

### Part 1 — Find the Differences
**Scene:** Two pictures of an outdoor picnic scene near a lake (tent, bikes, blanket)

**Examiner's opening:**
> *"Here are two pictures. My picture is similar to yours, but there are some differences."*
>
> *"For example, in my picture there's a girl on a rock in the lake, but in your picture there isn't."*
>
> *"I will say something about my picture. Can you tell me how your picture is different?"*

**5 differences (find after example):**

| Examiner says | Student should say |
|---|---|
| *"In my picture, two boys are sitting under a tree."* | *"In my picture, two girls are sitting under a tree."* |
| *"In my picture, the woman is eating a sandwich."* | *"In my picture, the woman is eating pizza."* |
| *"In my picture, there isn't a pizza on the blanket."* | *"In my picture there is a pizza on the blanket."* |
| *"In my picture, two boys are riding their bikes."* | *"In my picture, one boy is riding his bike."* |
| *"In my picture, a man is reading a newspaper outside the tent."* | *"In my picture, a man is reading a magazine outside the tent."* |

**Prompt questions (use only if student is silent):**
- *"Who is sitting under a tree in your picture?"*
- *"What is the woman eating?"*
- *"What's on the blanket?"*
- *"How many boys are riding their bikes?"*
- *"What is the man outside the tent reading?"*

### Part 2 — Information Exchange
**Context:** Richard and Harry are friends who go to school together.

**Examiner's opening:**
> *"Richard and Harry are friends. They go to school together. I don't know anything about Richard's programme. So I'm going to ask you some questions."*

**Examiner asks about Richard (student answers):**

| Examiner asks | Student's answer |
|---|---|
| *"What time does he get up in the morning?"* | At 7:00. |
| *"What time does he go to school?"* | At 8:00. |
| *"What does he do at 4:00 p.m.?"* | He does his homework. |
| *"What does he do at 7:00 p.m.?"* | He has a tennis lesson. |
| *"What time does he go to bed?"* | At 10:00. |

**Transition:**
> *"Now, you don't know anything about Harry's programme, so you ask me some questions."*

**Student asks about Harry (examiner answers):**

| Hint (shown to student) | Student should ask | Examiner answers |
|---|---|---|
| get up — time? | *"What time does he get up?"* | At 7:30. |
| go to school — time? | *"What time does he go to school?"* | At 8:15. |
| 2 o'clock — do? | *"What does he do at 2 o'clock?"* | He has lunch. |
| 6 p.m. — do? | *"What does he do at 6 p.m.?"* | He plays football. |
| go to bed — time? | *"What time does he go to bed?"* | At 10:30. |

### Part 3 — Tell the Story
**Story title:** *"Betty's visit to London"*

**Examiner describes Picture 1:**
> *"These pictures tell a story. It's called 'Betty's visit to London'. Look at the pictures first."*
>
> *"Betty, her brother Fred and their parents visit London. They arrive there on Monday and they go for a walk. They see Big Ben."*
>
> *"Now you tell the story."*

**Student continues (Pictures 2–5), one sentence at a time:**

**Picture 2** (Prompt: *"What day is it? What does the family do?"*)
1. "On Tuesday Betty and her family go shopping."
2. "They buy many presents and souvenirs."

**Picture 3** (Prompt: *"What day is it? What is Betty doing?"*)
1. "On Wednesday Betty writes a postcard to her grandmother."

**Picture 4** (Prompt: *"What day is it? Where is the family? What do they see? Is Betty afraid of the snakes? Who laughs?"*)
1. "On Thursday the family visits the zoo."
2. "They see a lot of animals there."
3. "Betty is afraid of the snakes and her brother Fred laughs."

**Picture 5** (Prompt: *"What day is it? Where is the family? What have they got with them? What does the father do?"*)
1. "On Friday they are at the airport."
2. "It is the last day in London."
3. "They have their bags with them."
4. "Betty's father takes pictures of the family."

### Part 4 — Personal Questions
**Topic:** Free time

> *"Now let's talk about what you do in your free time."*

1. *"What do you do after school?"*
2. *"How often do you go out with your friends?"*
3. *"What do you usually do at the weekend?"*
4. *"Do you play any sports?"*
5. *"What other things do you do in your free time?"*

**Follow-up prompts (student must answer all 4):**
- *"Do you play basketball or football?"*
- *"Do you go to the cinema?"*
- *"Do you play computer games with your friends?"*
- *"Do you help with the housework?"*

**Sample answers:** I do my homework. / Three times a week. / I visit my grandparents. / Yes, I play football. / I sometimes go to the cinema.

**Ending:** *"OK. Thank you, [name]. Goodbye."* → Student: *"Goodbye."*
